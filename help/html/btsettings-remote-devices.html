Remote Devices

Here you can discover all Bluetooth compatible devices in the near vicinity. Before you can communicate with another Bluetooth device, you will first need to discover it. If you plan to communicate with the remote device, you may also need to pair with the remote device.

When you first enter this screen, it will display a list of all your "Favorite" devices. These are the devices that have been paired with your device, and other devices that you have marked as Favorite devices. (To add or remove a device from your Favorites, select a device in the list and press the "Add to favorites" or "Remove from favorites" option.)

To perform a device search and find all devices in the vicinity, press the "Find more devices..." entry in the list of devices. The display will then show the devices that have been found in the vicinity, and indicate whether each device is paired with your device. The pairing is indicated by the icon. If a device is paired to your device, the checkbox will be ticked.

To view more information about a remote device:

  • First press enter to navigate to the list, and highlight the device you wish to examine. Make sure that you are selecting the first column. Press the OK button. A screen will appear showing detailed information about the device.

To pair a remote device:

  • First press OK to navigate to the list, and highlight the device you wish to pair. Navigate to the second column, and select the checkbox button. This starts the pairing process. A new dialog will appear, asking you to enter a pin. The pin can be alphanumeric, but some devices will only support numeric pins. You will need to enter the same number on the device that you are trying to pair to. For instructions on how to enter the pin on the remote device, please follow the manufacturer instructions for the remote device.

To send a business card:

  • First press OK to navigate to the list, and highlight the device you wish to communicate with. Select the Send Business Card option from the context menu.

To send a file:

  • First press OK to navigate to the list, and highlight the device you wish to communicate with. Make sure that you are selecting the first column. Select the Send File option from the context menu. A dialog will appear, prompting you for the file to send.
