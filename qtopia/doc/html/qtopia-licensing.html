Introduction

Qtopia depends on Qt/Embedded. There are two types of third-party software in Qt/Embedded -

  1. Software compiled as part of the Qt library itself - Qt Licenses
  2. Independent packages that may be added to enhance the capabilities of Qt - 3rd Party Qt Licenses

There are two basic categories of Qtopia licenses: commercial lincenses, and GPL-related licenses.

Commercial Licenses

The Qtopia commercial licenses are divided according to the package of which they are a part. See also Qtopia Packages.

Qtopia SDK Professional

The Qtopia SDK Professional is targetted at professional medium to large companies that carry out commercial Qtopia application development and who require the platform source code and full support and maintenance. The Commercial development SDK license enables developers to build commercial Qtopia applications for sale or free distribution. Commercial development SDK license holders are not required to pay runtime license fees. The Qtopia SDK Light comes with a Qt Duo pack (Qt/Embedded + Qt/Windows, Qt/Mac, or Qt/X11), the Qtopia library source as well as twelve months of support and maintenance. It is intended for professional companies that develop software for Qt/Embedded and/or Qtopia.

Qtopia Runtime License

To distribute Qtopia on consumer electronic devices you need to purchase Qtopia Runtime Licenses. You need one runtime license per devices. The cost per license depends on the number of runtime licenses purchased and the type of Qtopia product. A flexible target volume pricing is offered. The customer must commit to a minimum of 20% of the target volume.

Other License Issues

Qtopia itself also includes two other types of software. Trolltech original software licensed under:

Third-party software licensed under:

  1. the GNU General Public License (GPL Licensed Software).
  2. the GNU Lesser General Public License (LGPL Licensed Software).
  3. liberal licenses (Very Free Software).

For more information about Qtopia third party lincensing see Qtopia - Non-Trolltech licensed software.

Modifications

Note that if modifications are made to:

  • Dual Licensed Software, this may effect your right to redistribute GPL Licensed Software that links with the Dual Licensed Software, unless the modifications are made available under the terms of the GPL.
  • GPL Licensed Software, your right to redistribute that software is under the terms of the GPL.
  • LGPL Licensed Software, your right to redistribute that software is under the terms of the LGPL.
  • Very Free Software there are generally no constraints imposed upon redistribution.

A specific example case of Dual Licensed Software is the Media Player. The Media Player normally links with GPL Licensed Software such as the libmpeg3 plug-in, and therefore if you make modifications to the Media Player, you must either make those modifications under the terms of the GPL, or you must not distribute the GPL Licensed Software that links with the Media Player, such as the libmpeg3 plug-in.
